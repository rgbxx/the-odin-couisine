Mhadjeb
  • 60 minutes
  • 13 ingredients
Mahdjouba or Mhadjeb is a crepe-like semolina based flatbread originating from Algeria. Mhadjeb is a traditional Algerian dish, it is a fine crepe-like semolina based flatbread typically stuffed with a mixture of onion, garlic, tomato, peppers and spices.

Ingredients

  2. 1cup of thin semolina.
  3. pinch of salt.

Instructions
The dough:
  1. In a bowl mix thin semolina, flour, salt.
  2. After that you add water gradually(dont add it all at once it wont work).
  3. Knead with your hand until the dough is soft.
  4. The most important step is take a plastic bag and dip it in warm water.
  5. While the bag is still wet and warm(necessary) you put the dough in it and let it rest( 30mins necessary but the most the dough rests the better the results).
  6. After the dough has rested knead it lightly for couple minutes.
  7. Wet your hands with oil and form balls with the dough and put them next to each other while remembering the order.
  8. Let the balls rest for some time.
The filling:
  1. Cut the onions into thin slices and cook them in some oil over low heat until they become soft.
  2. Peel the tomatoes and chop them thinly then add them to the onions with the spices.
  3. Cover the pan but stirr occasionally so it doesnt stick.
Shaping mhajeb:
  1. Over and oiled work space put the first ball of dough. sprinkle lightly with oil to help u form a big circle.
  2. Put the filling in the middle(filling must be cold).
  3. Fold over each side to enclose the stuffind and get a square.
  4. Lightly oil the surface of the square and gently put it on a baking sheet or a pan over medium-low heat.
  5. Cook the first side well browned turn over and do the same to the other one.
  6. Repeat till you finish all the balls.
Enjoy it hot!
